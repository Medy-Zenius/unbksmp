Butterflies are some of the World's most wondrous animals. Their beauty is seemingly a miraculous metamorphosis and apparently a carefree flight and all spark our imaginations.

As advanced insects, butterflies and moths have a "complete" life cycle. This means that there are four separate stages, each of which looks completely different and serves a different purpose in the life of the insect. They are eggs, caterpillars, chrysalises, and colorful butterflies.

Butterflies and moths are found in all continents except Antarctic, and scientists estimate that there are approximately 12-15,000 species of butterflies. In the United States and Canada, more than 750 species of butterflies have been recorded. Many species of a few kinds of butterflies are still being discovered.
