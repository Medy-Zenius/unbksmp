A Rabbit and Twenty Crocodiles
Once upon a time, a rabbit wanted to cross a river, but he could not swim. After a while, he had an idea. He saw a boss of crocodiles swimming in the river. The rabbit asked him, "How many crocodile are there in the river?"
The boss of crocodile answered, "We are twenty here".
"Where are they?" the rabbit asked for the second time.
"What is it for?" the boss crocodile asked.
"The king of the jungle will grant a big prize because of your good, gentle and kind manner. I want to know the exact number of the crocodile here, so please make a line in order," said the rabbit.
Then the boss of the Crocodiles called all his friends and asked them to do what the rabbit wanted. They make a line from one side to the other side of the river. After that, the rabbit started to count while jumping from one crocodile to another: one .... two .... three .... four .... until twenty. And finally, he thanked all of them because he had successfully crossed the river.
