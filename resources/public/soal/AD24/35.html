Guava is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical countries for it is an edible fruit. Guava fruit, usually 2 to 4 inches long, is round or oval depending on the species. Varying between species, the skin can be any thickness, it is usually green when unripe, but it becomes yellow or maroon when it is ripe. The flesh of Guava fruit is sweet or sour. The color of the flesh may be white,pink,yellow, or red, with the seeds in the central part of the flesh. The seeds are numerous but small. In some varieties, they are edible. Actual seed counts have ranged from 112 to 535. Guava fruit is rich with vitamins A and C, omega-3 and omega-6, fatty acids and high levels of dietary fiber.
